About the artist

where the pencil meets technology

My passion has always been art, I love pushing concepts further than what is expected. I like working with people to gather ideas to build something extraordinary. One of my favorite mediums I still fall back to, is traditional graphite. I usually fall back on traditional art to gain quick layouts or sketches that get rendered into final pieces. I use traditional sketches primarily because I've worked them for so many years.

What has been really interesting is being able to combine traditional art with digital mediums. What has helped me a lot is having a really good foundation in design and art to able to input that into a digital format that produces amazing results. The best work I've completed is when I utilize both techniques traditional and digital.

Front End fear

I got involed with front-end developing when I was really immersing myself in graphic design. I wanted to take art a step further. Front-end developing does that because they are the real sketch artists behind the screens when it comes to computers.

One big fear I had when I was learning how to code was messing things up. I didn,t want to break anything I could not fix but I needed to overcome that fear otherwise I would never learn. I ended up overcoming that fear by not giving up until I achieved the results, I wanted no matter how long it took. After overcoming this fear, it has allowed me to see the endless possibilities that can be done with front end development.
